Pointless 27: Czechia

Pointless rules

  • This is a trivia game that tests how obscure you can get. The goal is to get the fewest points.
  • Please refrain from consulting any outside sources from this point onward. Only use your own knowledge base to answer questions.

Game-specific rules

  • You may provide up to 20 answers for each question, separated by newlines. (I do not expect you to provide this many to any of the questions; the limit is mostly to preserve my sanity.)
  • All answers after your second incorrect answer will be discarded.
  • The score of each remaining correct answer is the number of other players who gave that answer. A wrong answer is scored as the number of players who gave the most common correct answer plus 1.
  • A penalty of (0‑indexed position of the answer in your list) ÷ 2 will then be applied. For example, if your fourth answer was given by two other players, it is worth 3.5 points.
  • Your score for a question is the minimum of your answer scores.
  • You may not pass; instead, your highest score will be dropped.
  • There will be both per-section and overall leaderboards.

There are 5 sections, with 3 questions each.

Science fiction · Vědeckofantastický žánr

The word "robot" was coined by Karel Čapek in the 1921 scifi play "Rossumovi Univerzální Roboti" ("Rossum's Universal Robots"). It comes from the Czech word "robota", meaning roughly "drudgery"/"servitude".

Name any specific robot from a piece of media categorized as "science fiction". The robot must have a proper name (e.g. "medical nanobot from X" is invalid), must be intelligent or anthropomorphic in some way (not just a machine), and must be completely nonliving (cyborgs and the like don't count).

Board games · Deskové hry

Vladimír Chvátil is a well-known board game designer who was born in Czechoslovakia. His most famous creation is probably Codenames, which won the Spiel des Jahres (German for "Game of the Year") award in 2016. In Codenames, two teams of players compete to communicate and guess a set of words the fastest.

Name any other board or card game played in teams. The teams must compete with one another, such that one team is declared the winner (or they tie) at the end of the game. There must be at least two teams, and at least one team must have multiple members.

example: "Codenames" (you may not answer with these examples)

Section 3Academia · Akademická sféra

Mathematics · Matematika

Eduard Čech, a mathematician from Bohemia, is best known for his work on what are now known as Stone-Čech compactification and Čech cohomology.

Name a term in mathematics that begins with "co", where "co" is not a prefix. That is, "cohomology" is invalid, because it is derived directly from "co-" + "homology". Terms which are technically "co-" plus another word, but where the other word is unrelated to the term's meaning, are fine. Etymologically derived terms, like "compact" and "compactification", will be considered the same.

example: "compact"="compactification" (you may not answer with these examples)

invalid example: "cohomology" (co- is a prefix)

source: Wikipedia, MathWorld, nLab (your answer will count iff it is contained in the title of an article on at least 1 of these)

Linguistics · Lingvistika

The English word "Czech" is borrowed from the Polish word "Czech", hence the spelling -- in Polish, <cz> stands for a sound similar to the first sound in "cheese", and <ch> stands for a sound similar to the last sound in "Bach" (in some dialects).

Name any digraph -- that is, a pair of characters used to write a different sound than the two characters individually -- in the Latin alphabet, along with the language it's used in. The language part will be ignored for scoring purposes (so "ch / English" counts as the same as "ch / Polish"), but it must be correct for your answer to be accepted. Your answer may not contain letters with diacritical marks (e.g. ã, ú, ø, č).

example: "cz / Polish", "ch / English"="ch / Polish" (you may not answer with these examples)

source: "List of Latin-script digraphs"

History · Dějepis

Throughout Bohemian history, there were three notable instances in which people were thrown out of windows for political reasons, now known as the Defenestrations of Prague. The third had the greatest impact on history, inciting the Thirty Years' War, in which an estimated 3 million to 11.5 million people died.

Name any other war whose estimated death toll is at least 1 million.

example: "Thirty Years' War" (you may not answer with these examples)
