This is a database of all currently known published results (to my knowledge) about the computational complexity of grid-based pencil-and-paper Nikoli-style logic puzzles.

If you know of any papers missing from this collection, please let me know!

Here is a brief explanation of this website's iconography.

  • The icon marks reductions for which I have embedded interactive copies of the gadgets used in the reduction! Click on the row to see them. You can draw on the gadgets with your mouse to see how they work, or click on the states in the parentheses above them to view the partially- or fully-solved states.
  • Many logic puzzle genres have been invented, and there are many online sources that catalog them. Hovering over a puzzle name will also show the set of sources that use that name, and clicking the icons to the right of a puzzle name will take you to that puzzle in the corresponding source.
    • pzv: pzprxs, a fork of puzz.link, itself a fork of PUZ-PRE; together the primary online interfaces used to solve Nikoli-style puzzles
    • tatham: Simon Tatham's Portable Puzzle Collection
    • cross+a: Cross+A, a proprietary desktop application
    • janko: the Rätselübersicht, a collection by German puzzlers Angela and Otto Janko
    • other: any puzzle type not catalogued in the above
  • Click on the icon to copy a BibTeX citation for the paper to your clipboard.

To filter the table, you can click on the controls at the top of the screen. Here is a brief explanation of each control:

  • The "filter puzzles" dropdown allows you to select which sources of puzzle genres to draw from (among those mentioned above).
  • The following set of four checkboxes filters puzzles and results by whether they show ASP-completeness, NP-completeness, polynomial time solvability, or if the puzzle's complexity is open.
  • The "results" filter has three options. With the "best" setting, at most one result is shown, with ASP-completeness results prioritized over NP-completeness results (and otherwise tiebreaking by earliest year). With the "all" setting, all results are shown. With the "interactive" setting, only results for which I have embedded interactive versions of the gadgets are included.
  • In the top right, you will see some basic summary statistics about how many entries there are in the database matching the filters you have selected.
