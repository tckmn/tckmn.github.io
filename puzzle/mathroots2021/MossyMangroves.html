You notice some scraps of paper in a drawer of the desk beside you, and upon closer examination discover something very curious. Apparently, Merlin is very peculiar about the trees surrounding his castle. For one thing, he seems to insist that they align to a strict rectangular grid, and for another, he keeps very close track of their roots...

Of course, he doesn't just draw them in directly. Instead, it seems like the numbers on the outsides of the grid indicate how many grid cells contain either a tree or a part of their roots. By some miracle he's convinced the trees to grow their roots perfectly aligned to his grid, and by an even greater miracle all of his trees have exactly 3 roots which span a distance of 2 grid cells each. So this is enough information to determine the root placement entirely.
